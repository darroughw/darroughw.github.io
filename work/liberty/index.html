Liberty Hardware Shopify Platform

Role: Lead Design / DevelopmentYear: 2025
Web DesignDesign SystemUX ResearchResponsiveLiquidAIOSEO

Overview

Liberty Hardware, a leading cabinet hardware manufacturer, needed their Shopify storefront rebuilt from the ground up. The existing site buried products in navigation that hadn't scaled with the catalog, and it was failing to convert the professional buyers who make up the bulk of the business.

The Problem

1. Product discovery was broken.

The catalog had grown past 4,000 SKUs, but the navigation and search experience hadn't grown with it. Buyers who already knew what they needed still had to fight the site to find it, and every extra step between landing page and product page cost conversions.

2. The visual and technical system couldn't scale.

Product presentation was inconsistent across those 4,000+ SKUs, varying by category depending on who had built the page last. Mobile performance degraded under product filtering, the exact moment buyers needed the site to be fast. And SEO had been an afterthought, so a manufacturer with real product depth wasn't showing up for the searches that mattered.

What I Did

A design system for the storefront

I built a comprehensive Shopify Liquid design system to bring consistency across the catalog: reusable templates, components, and content patterns so pages didn't have to be reinvented for every product category.

Faceted search architecture

The core of the discovery fix was a faceted search architecture that let professional buyers filter down to the exact product instead of browsing for it. That addressed the discovery failure directly: search became a tool for buyers who already knew what they wanted, not a fallback for buyers who didn't.

Photography and content standards

I set smart product photography guidelines to standardize how the catalog looked page to page, and built an AIO-optimized content structure so pages would perform in both traditional search and the newer generation of AI-driven answer engines.

Cross-functional execution

None of this happened in isolation. I worked directly with engineering on the technical build and with marketing on content and photography standards throughout the rebuild.

Results

60+
Target terms ranking page 1

Conversion and session duration are demand-side signals: they only move when the underlying experience gets easier to use, not just easier to look at. Ranking on page 1 for 60+ target terms is the supply-side half of the same fix, proof that the content structure earned trust from search engines. Together, they show the rebuild solved discovery from both directions: whether someone found the site through search, or landed on it and needed to find a product once they were there.
